# Worked Example Outline

This worked example is intentionally small and role-based. It shows that the workflow is not trying to force one visual format onto every paragraph.

- `sample-3-pro-01.png` demonstrates a process explainer role
- `sample-3-pro-02.png` demonstrates a slot-planning comparison role
- `sample-3-pro-03.png` demonstrates a scene-led editorial cover role
- `example-article.md` is the source text used to derive the locked facts below

## Illustration 1

- Section anchor: `2. Plan Image Slots`
- Visual goal: explain the article-to-visual workflow at a glance
- Locked facts:
  - the workflow starts by extracting verified claims from the article
  - it plans image slots before any generation
  - it generates one image at a time and reviews fidelity
- Visual type: infographic / process explainer
- Aspect: `16:9`
- Filename: `sample-3-pro-01.png`
- Notes: keep the image text-light and use only three short labels

## Illustration 2

- Section anchor: `2. Plan Image Slots`
- Visual goal: compare three valid slot outcomes for one article section
- Locked facts:
  - not every paragraph needs a picture
  - some sections want a flowchart
  - some need a framework card
  - some are better left text-only
- Visual type: comparison card
- Aspect: `4:3`
- Filename: `sample-3-pro-02.png`
- Notes: keep the card text-light and allow only the three column labels

## Illustration 3

- Section anchor: `From Article To Illustration Pack`
- Visual goal: provide one quiet section-cover visual for the overall article
- Locked facts:
  - good article visuals clarify the writing instead of distracting from it
  - the workflow reads the article first
  - image slots are decided later
- Visual type: scene-led editorial cover
- Aspect: `3:4`
- Filename: `sample-3-pro-03.png`
- Notes: keep the image text-free and let the paper page plus three planning frames imply the workflow

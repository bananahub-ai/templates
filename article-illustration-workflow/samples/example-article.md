# From Article To Illustration Pack

Good article visuals should clarify the writing instead of distracting from it. The safest workflow is to read the article first, extract a few verified claims, and only then decide what kind of image each section actually needs.

## 1. Extract Verified Claims

Before generating anything, compress the source into a short list of claims or section goals. The image should be anchored to those verified facts instead of a vague mood board.

## 2. Plan Image Slots

Not every paragraph needs a picture. Some sections want a flowchart, some need a framework card, and some are better left text-only. Planning image slots before generation keeps the visual pack compact and coherent.

## 3. Generate One Image At A Time

Generate one image, review it for fidelity, and only then move to the next slot. If an image looks attractive but adds unsupported ideas, regenerate from the locked facts instead of stacking more style instructions on top.
